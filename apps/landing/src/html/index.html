Frequent Asked Questions

What's the minimum I can stake? There is no minimum! However, there is a point at which gas fees make very small amounts staked not worth it.
How are rewards generated? Casimir combines users' ETH into 32 ETH pools and forms validator groups with 4 operators using Distributed Validator Technology, generating rewards distributed in proportion to users' shares in the pool.
How does Casimir work? What does it mean to create an account? Casimir uses distributed validator tech to pool users' ETH into 32 ETH pools across four node operators. Users maintain custody through DKG, and rewards are distributed in native ETH based on their pool share.
What are the advantages of staking with Casimir over other protocols? Casimir enables users to stake any amount of ETH while keeping custody and earning rewards in native ETH. It combines the benefits of liquid and solo staking with added security through multiple validators (DVT). Additionally, staking native ETH provides access to exclusive solutions like Eigenlayer.
What are the risks? Although there is a risk of smart contract vulnerabilities, Casimir minimizes this risk through open-source code, audits, and a bug bounty program. Validators/operators may face penalties, but thanks to Distributed Validator Technology (DVT), funds are not lost in case of their failure.
Who is behind Casimir? Are the smart contracts audited and open source? Casimir is developed by Consensus Networks, a US-based team with extensive experience in the crypto space. The team has a history of early participation as node operators in various PoS protocols and has received grants from Web3 and government sources for digital asset management tool development.
What is Distributed Validator Technology (DVT)? DVT enhances Ethereum's validation layer by distributing validator operations to multiple non-trusting nodes (Operators). These operator clusters run validators on behalf of stakers and address centralization, redundancy, and security challenges inherent in Ethereum's PoS consensus.
Why don’t you use a Liquid Staking Derivative or Token (LSD or LST)? Liquidity Staking Derivatives (LSDs) come with inherent risks, notably counterparty risk. As LSDs essentially function as IOUs, insolvency of the issuer could result in the permanent loss of the user's ETH.
Are there ways I can use my staked ETH for additional yield? Although Casimir is not launching an LSD, there are opportunities for stakers to provide their native staked ETH as liquidity providers (e.g., to support a DAO). Additionally, we will be introducing restaking mechanisms like Eigenlayer that will enable users to natively restake their ETH to maximize their APR, if desired.
Am I giving up custody of my staked ETH? No! You will always be able to withdraw your staked ETH from your smart contract.
How frequently do I get my rewards? Rewards will be distributed as they are awarded by the Ethereum protocol, at least daily.
Where are my rewards distributed, is there a secondary token? Your rewards will be distributed to your wallet, there is no need to deal with the hassle of swapping a token to get your rewards.
What Wallets are supported? Today we support Metamask, Wallet Connect, Trezor, Ledger, Trust Wallet and Coinbase Wallet
Is there a lockup period or waiting period for withdrawals? Since you are staking native ETH, there may be a wait time for a validator to exit to receive your withdrawal. If you desire a quicker exit, we will soon be launching a native ETH liquidity pool for those who wish for an instant withdrawal.
Where is my stake going, who is running the validators? Casimir begins with a small set of validators but will soon open up for anyone with a 1 ETH collateral. Validator performance is overseen by a decentralized oracle network, with performance specs used in operator selection.
What is the fee? There is a small fee, paid for by part of the block rewards to cover validator and other network payments to ensure Casimir keeps running.
Can I run a validator? If you have the technical ability and interest, you'll soon have the chance to become a validator in our protocol. While we're initially launching with a small group of white-listed validators, we'll soon open up to everyone. Stay updated by joining our Discord.
